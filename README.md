The Retail Store Sales and Inventory Analysis project was developed using Microsoft Power BI to evaluate sales performance, inventory efficiency, product demand patterns, seasonal trends, and operational effectiveness across a multi-location retail chain. The primary objective was to identify factors contributing to inventory imbalances, stockouts, excess inventory, and inconsistent sales performance while providing data-driven recommendations to improve profitability and inventory management.

The analysis was conducted on a dataset containing 9,994 retail transaction records. Data cleaning, transformation, and modeling were performed to ensure data quality before developing interactive dashboards and key performance indicators (KPIs) for business decision-making.

Key Business Metrics
The dashboard revealed the following organizational performance metrics:

5,009 total orders processed
$2.3 million total sales revenue
$286,000+ total profit generated
37,873 units sold
$458.60 Average Order Value (AOV)
These KPIs provided a comprehensive overview of the company's operational and financial performance.

Key Findings and Insights
Sales Performance Trends
Sales revenue demonstrated a steady upward trajectory from 2014 to 2017, increasing from approximately $484,000 to over $733,000, indicating sustained business growth and expanding customer demand.

Regional Performance Analysis
Significant geographical disparities were identified. California emerged as the highest-performing market, generating approximately $458,000 in sales, followed by New York and Texas. Conversely, several states contributed minimal revenue, highlighting opportunities for targeted regional marketing and inventory optimization.

Inventory Efficiency
Inventory turnover analysis revealed that Furniture and Office Supplies categories experienced the highest turnover rates, while Technology products remained in inventory longer despite generating substantial revenue. This finding highlighted opportunities to optimize procurement and replenishment strategies.

Product Demand Analysis
Fast-moving products such as Staples, Staple Envelopes, and Easy-Staple Paper showed consistent customer demand, while several specialty products demonstrated slow movement patterns, increasing inventory carrying costs.

Seasonality and Holiday Impact
Holiday periods accounted for approximately 68% of total sales, with Back-to-School, Black Friday, Easter, and Christmas emerging as major revenue-driving events. Seasonal analysis also showed that Fall and Winter generated the highest sales volumes, emphasizing the importance of proactive inventory planning.

Stockout Risk Assessment
The analysis identified a stockout frequency of 3.35% among high-demand products, indicating potential revenue loss and customer dissatisfaction due to delayed replenishment processes.

Revenue Drivers
Technology was the highest revenue-generating category, while Chairs and Phones were identified as the most influential sub-categories. These products significantly contributed to overall business profitability and sales growth.

Promotion Effectiveness
Results showed that higher discount levels did not always translate into proportional revenue growth, suggesting diminishing returns from excessive discounting and highlighting the need for more targeted promotional strategies.

Operational Performance
Shipping analysis revealed that Same-Day delivery offered the fastest fulfillment times, while Standard Class shipping experienced the longest delivery durations. This insight supports initiatives aimed at improving customer satisfaction through optimized logistics.

Business Recommendations
Based on the analysis, several strategic recommendations were proposed:

Implement demand forecasting models to improve inventory planning and reduce stockouts.
Align inventory allocation with regional demand patterns and seasonal trends.
Increase investment in high-performing product categories and locations.
Prioritize replenishment of fast-moving products while reducing procurement of slow-moving inventory.
Optimize promotional strategies by focusing on targeted discounts rather than aggressive price reductions.
Strengthen holiday inventory planning and staffing to capitalize on peak demand periods.
Adopt predictive analytics and real-time inventory monitoring systems to improve operational efficiency and profitability.
